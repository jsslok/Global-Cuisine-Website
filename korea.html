Key Characteristics of Korean Cuisine

Balance of Favours

Korean dishes often strive for a balance of five flavors: sweet, sour, salty, bitter, and spicy. This balance is believed to promote health and well-being.

Fermented Food

Fermentation plays a significant role in Korean cuisine. Foods like kimchi, doenjang (soybean paste), and gochujang (red chili paste) not only add depth of flavor but also offer health benefits through probiotics.

Variety of Side Dishes

A typical Korean meal includes an array of side dishes, or banchan, which accompany the main dishes. These can range from simple steamed vegetables to elaborate preparations like seasoned seaweed or marinated beef.

Rice and Grains

Rice is the staple of Korean meals, often complemented by other grains like barley and millet. It serves as a neutral base that allows the vibrant flavors of side dishes and main courses to shine.
